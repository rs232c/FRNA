Which athletes should be Herald News Football Player of the Year?

Herald News December 06, 2025 at 09:24 PM

FOOTBALL Which athletes should be Herald News Football Player of the Year? Steven Sanchez The Herald News Updated Dec. 5, 2025, 4:44 p.m. ET Who was the Greater Fall River area's top high school football player this fall? The Herald News is letting readers have a say in the winner in the Fan's Choice poll. Area athletes put on a display all season and into the postseason. These individuals stood out from the rest, and it's time to give them the recognition they deserve. Readers can vote in the poll until Dec. 14, and results will be combined with staff votes to determine the Football Player of the Year. Here's a look at the 12 nominees below. Marvins Antoine, Durfee The Hilltopper senior running back had a solid season in 2025. He was among the team leaders in rushing yards and had 8 touchdowns on the season. Antoine rushed for over 100 yards on Thanksgiving and scored 3 touchdowns. Nathaniel Benevides, Atlantis Charter/Westport Benevides was one of the most talented players on the field for the Tritons this past fall. He had a team-best 6 touchdowns and points scored (36). Aedan Borges, Joseph Case Borges had another outstanding season for the Cardinals this past fall. He lead all area players in points scored (113) and had 14 touchdowns, 13 extra points and 4 field goals. He helped lead Case to its first postseason appearance since 2019. Moziah Cousins, Durfee Cousins had an excellent season from the running back spot for the Hilltoppers. He rushed for 8 touchdowns, which was second on the team. Cousins had a 3 touchdown game this fall. Alexi Gonzalez, Diman Gonzalez was the work horse for the Bengals all season long. He recorded 8 touchdowns on the year, including 2 against Whittier in the Mass. Vocational Large School Super Bowl. Gonzalez also rushed for a game-high 166 yards. AJ Griffin, Somerset Berkley Griffin was part of the Raiders' three-headed monster in the backfield. He scored 3 touchdowns and converted a pair of two-point conversion. Griffin had a season-best 100 yards in the pre-Thanksgiving Day game against Central High. James McKenzie, Diman The Bengals most consistent player of both offense and defense this fall. McKenzie scored a team-best 9 touchdowns and 56 points. He had an interception in the Mass. Vocational Large School Super Bowl. Logan McKenzie, Diman The Bengals signal caller came up big in key moments of the season. He threw for an area-best 15 touchdowns and rushed for another 7 touchdowns. He completed nine passes for 191 yards in the Mass. Vocational Large School Super Bowl. Liam Meehan, Somerset Berkley The Raiders senior running back went over the 1,000-yard mark and had a career and area-best rushing game (316) against Dighton-Rehoboth. Meehan has a area-best 18 touchdowns and added a pair of two-pointers. Aaron Sellers, Joseph Case Sellers' first season under center was a successful one for the Cardinals in 2025. He threw for five touchdowns and helped lead Case to its first postseason berth since 2019. Matt Sherry, Durfee Sherry had another great season for the Hilltoppers at the quarterback position. He had a team-best 102, scored 14 touchdowns and threw for four touchdowns. Sherry also added an area-best 8 two-pointers and kicked 10 extra points. Collyn Viveiros, Atlantis Charter/Westport Viveiros was one of the go to person on the field for the Tritons. He finished with 5 touchdowns on the season. Viveiros was a solid defender on defense.
